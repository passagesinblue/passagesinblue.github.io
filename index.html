Passages in Blue

A Public Diary

30th of March, 2018

I'm in a little town near the west coast of Ireland.

I came down from Dublin yesterday by train.

Walking to the train station I had the idea that I would set up a website in order to improve my web development skills.

Then this afternoon after getting in from a visit to a supermarket, I thought that it might be interesting to people for them to read a public diary of my life, though that certainly sounds narcissistic now.

Nevertheless, here we are.

It will be anonymous but otherwise honest. Nothing too cringey but hopefully somewhat insightful.

My mother said to me yesterday that I could make a good counsellor because I seem detacted when she talks to me.

It's a career that would interest me but I dislike the thought of committing time to study and to work.

Writing I prefer.

I think I inherited a talent for writing through her side of the family.

Her sister could write well and my mother herself has been complimented by her peers for her letters and messages in cards.

I'll be here in the west for a few days. Easter weekend and beyond.

I'm bothered by inability to give this website a suitable name. I was thinking of 'A Passage In Time' but really that's just piggybacking on Dead Can Dance's eye for the poetic.

Passages In Time perhaps?

Still derivative and now leaning towards Al Stewart's Time Passages.

I have often struggled with names and piviotal words.

Looking back on this even it seems pretentious drivel. Oh well, perhaps it will improve. Unfortunate for you reader and me also.

I think writing or working on what one believes in most is the way to work well and often.

But I am probably wrong. It's an attachment, something that seems huge and profound but only because I hold it so close.
